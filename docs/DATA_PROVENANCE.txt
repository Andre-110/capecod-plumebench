CapeCod-PlumeBench Data Provenance
==================================

This repository contains an offline deterministic benchmark inspired by the
USGS Cape Cod treated-wastewater groundwater plume monitoring program. The
files in `data/` are not a live download from USGS. They are a physics-inspired
synthetic derivative designed for SE-Bench style hidden evaluation.

Why synthetic derivative data are used:

- The benchmark must be reproducible without network access.
- The public workspace must not contain hidden target concentrations.
- The judge must keep hidden future scenarios, hidden wells, hidden plume
  metrics, and hidden candidate monitoring utilities outside the agent-visible
  workspace.
- The task should preserve the real research workflow: well chemistry, plume
  migration in a sand-and-gravel aquifer, future risk prediction, and monitoring
  campaign design.

Public files:

- `data/public_wells.csv`: 60 public well locations and screen depths.
- `data/public_observations.csv`: 4183 public chemistry observations from
  1978-2018.
- `data/prediction_requests.csv`: 864 future prediction requests over 36 target
  wells, 6 future years, and 4 analytes.
- `data/plume_metric_requests.csv`: 8 nitrate/chloride plume-scale metric
  requests.
- `data/candidate_monitoring_wells.csv`: 40 candidate monitoring wells.
- `data/public_site_config.json`: units, thresholds, analyte parameters, and
  monitoring budget.

Hidden judge files are not included in this public repository. In the SE-Bench
task image they are generated inside the judge image under `/opt/capecod_hidden`
and are readable only by the scorer.

Reference context:

USGS data release title:
"Chemical Data From 40 Years of Monitoring a Treated-Wastewater Groundwater
Plume in a Sand and Gravel Aquifer, Cape Cod, Massachusetts, 1978-2018"

This benchmark is an offline synthetic derivative of that monitoring setting,
not a redistribution of the full original USGS dataset.
